## What I do

I specialize in identifying "Mixed Signal" accounts—profiles that possess legitimate technical expertise or share valuable resources but fail to meet the reciprocity standards of a "High Value" relationship builder. I distinguish these accounts from "Nice-Thought" accounts by recognizing verifiable technical substance, while docking points for self-centric or broadcast-only behaviors.

## When to use me

Use me when an account exhibits a conflict between **Technical Content Quality** and **Community Engagement Style**:
- The user shares repos, papers, or code (High Value signal) BUT only amplifies their own content (Nice-Thought signal).
- The user provides deep technical insights (High Value signal) BUT only replies to mega-influencers/celebs or never replies at all (Nice-Thought signal).
- The account is a "One-Way Broadcaster": high informational utility, zero conversational utility.

## Guidelines

To assign a **MODERATE** (50-70%) score, apply the following logic:

1. **Verify the "Value" Floor**:
   - Ensure the account shares **original, verifiable work** (repos, deployed apps, original research) or high-quality curated resources.
   - If there is no technical evidence, downgrade to **LOW** or **SKIP**.

2. **Apply the "Reciprocity Penalty"**:
   - Deduct significant points if the account **never** replies to smaller accounts (<10K followers).
   - Deduct points if the timeline is >90% self-promotion (links to their own blog/product) without commentary on others' work.
   - Deduct points if the account engages exclusively with "viral" tweets from industry giants without engaging in niche discussions.

3. **Scoring Thresholds**:
   - **65-70% (High Moderate)**: Deep technical substance, but mostly self-promotional or unresponsive to comments. Worth following for content, not for relationship.
   - **50-65% (Low Moderate)**: Good curation or decent code snippets, but high "engagement farming" tendencies (e.g., "Like/Retweet if you agree") or generic replies to famous accounts.

4. **Common Pitfalls to Avoid**:
   - Do not score as **HIGH_VALUE** just because they share code. HIGH_VALUE requires community amplification.
   - Do not score as **LOW** just because they self-promote. LOW implies "slop" or no value. If the product/code is real, the floor is MODERATE.
